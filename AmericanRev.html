How the Protestant Reformation Influenced the American Revolution

During the Protestant Reformation, many Puritans started to move to the Americas in search of
religious freedom. They didn't like how the churches in England were too Catholic. An example
is the Mayflower, where Puritans were treated badly by King James I, so they sailed to America.
This mindset of wanting freedom and independence later on spread to the American Revolution!
The 13 colonies started to form because of this. This is how people, ideas, and events all come
together, as the Puritans (people) had their own beliefs (ideas), and as a result moved to the
Americas (event) and with other protestant or catholics, created colonies. But then the
American Revolution happened because people were mad about the taxes and tyrant-like king.

How England's Constitutional Monarchy & Rulers Influenced the American Revolution

Throughout England's history, a lot of issues with rulers occured. This was mainly due to religious disagreement between Catholics and Protestants. For example, Edward VI was protestant, but when he died his sister Mary I came to throne and forced Roman Catholicism to churches. She was brutal to Protestants, and executed many, which led her to be known as "Bloody Mary". Many protestants wanted to rebel and stop Mary from marrying Phillip II of Spain, because both were Catholic. So, the Wyatt's rebellion occured where Thomas Wyatt led a lot of men to protest and attempt to get people to join the group. Although some rebellions like this one failed, it generally motivated people to "fight-back" and be independent, and overthrow evil and corrupt rulers.

Later on, England became a constitutional monarchy instead of an absolute monarchy. This meant the rulers had less power because they too had laws they needed to follow. A Bill of Rights was created in 1689, similar to how the Bill of Rights with 10 amendments were created after the American Revolution. The Bill of Rights included the below:

  • No suspending/not following parliament laws
  • No levying of taxes without grant from parliament
  • No interfering with freedom of speech in Parliament
  • No penalty for citizens who protested/voted against the king

How the Enlightenment & Scientific Revolution Influenced the American Revolution

The enlightenment was a time period after the Scientific Revolution, but before the American Revolution, where people were already thinking scientifically and reasoning instead, and the ideas during the enlightenment influenced the colonists during the American Revolution such as independence, people mattering more, and better governments which were more fair to the people, as colonists were mad that the British government taxed them, but didn't listen their voices. People during this time period were starting to challege authorities and question their actions because they didn't only care about religion anymore, and they questioned corrupt churches.

John Adams was a founding father and the 2nd president of the U.S. During the Enlightenment, he advocated for America's independence from Britain. He helped on things like signing and creating Declaration of Independence, was the governor of Massachusetts, and helped George Washington by establishing the navy for the Continental Army.

John Locke was a key person who influenced the thought of fighting for natural rights and overthrowing a government that wasn't fair and didn't protect the people. If the government didn't protect the people, why should they lose some of their rights to them? The colonists used this idea to help create the Declaration of Independence.

Baron de Montesquieu was a French philosopher and judge who influenced the U.S. constitution by his idea where government is seperated into different powers like the legislative, judicial, and executive branches, all serving different roles like creating laws, passing them through the judicial branch to be judged, and then actually enforcing them through the executive branch.
